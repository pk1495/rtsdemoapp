South Wedge neighborhood
Monroe Ave has been Rochester's bohemian corridor since the 1970s. The South Wedge was one of the city's first neighborhoods to see student-led revitalization, with UR and RIT grads opening cafés and studios along this stretch in the late 1980s.
Did you know?
The Goodman St intersection was once a streetcar hub in the early 1900s before the city's trolley lines were decommissioned in 1941 — replaced by the very buses you're riding today.
